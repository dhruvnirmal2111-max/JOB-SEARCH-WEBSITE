# Electronic Arts (EA) — Data Scientist, Publishing Insights & Markets (Asia Publishing)

> Source: owner-provided posting. Captured 2026-07-07. Team: Publishing Insights and Markets (EA Asia Publishing). Works alongside data engineers/scientists/analysts in Asia and globally. (Location not stated in JD, likely Asia-based/hybrid — confirm eligibility.)

## About the role
Perform quantitative analysis of internal and external datasets; build and maintain statistical and ML models that recommend actions to improve player experiences. Identify opportunities in priority markets, understand what drives player and market behaviours, and build trend-detection tools/dashboards.

## Key responsibilities
- Expand, enhance and maintain **segmentation models** to create customer personas for priority games and markets.
- Expand, enhance and maintain **forecasting models** for expected marketplace behaviours/performance.
- Perform thorough **testing and validation** of models to maintain accuracy.
- Explore first- and third-party datasets for quantitative analysis; surface relevant metrics and unseen trends.
- Work with analysts to align data science outputs to business requirements and contribute to the **automation of distributed reports** using DS outputs.
- Write and maintain **documentation** for assigned models and pipelines.
- Proactively learn new tools in data/ML/AI; contribute to team ways of working.

## Requirements
- Tertiary education in Data Science, Statistics, Computer Science, or equivalent practical experience.
- 2+ years in a data science role; Python or R.
- Statistical and ML fundamentals and common modelling techniques, and their trade-offs.
- Familiarity with SQL and databases.
- Familiarity with data visualisation tools (Power BI, Looker, Tableau).
- Strong problem-solving and communication.

## Nice to have
- Familiarity with EA games / similar games / gaming in general.
- ML or statistical models applied across a variety of commercial environments.
- Good code-repository practices.
- Libraries: pandas, scikit-learn, TensorFlow.
- Agile methodologies.
