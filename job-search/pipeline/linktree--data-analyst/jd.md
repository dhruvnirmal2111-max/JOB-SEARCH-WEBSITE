# Data Analyst (Business Analytics, L3) — Linktree

> Source: pasted via /apply on 2026-07-15.

## About the company
Linktree is the market-leading link-in-bio platform used by tens of millions of creators, with billions of monthly visitors. The Business Analytics team sits at the intersection of product, growth, and leadership, turning that firehose of creator and visitor data into the answers and insights teams use to decide what to build, who to reach, and where to grow next.

## The Role
As a Data Analyst on the Business Analytics team, you'll partner directly with Product Managers, Marketers, and senior leaders to size opportunities, instrument new launches, diagnose what's working (and what isn't), and shape the metrics that anchor the roadmap. Your work won't sit in a dashboard nobody opens; it shows up in roadmap decisions, leadership reviews, and the experiments that move north-star numbers.

This is an **L3 role** for someone ready to operate independently: owning analyses end-to-end, framing the right question before touching SQL, and being the trusted analytical partner inside a product squad.

## What You'll Own
- Partner with a Product squad as the embedded analyst: sizing opportunities, instrumenting launches, evaluating experiments, and making sure the team's decisions are grounded in the actual data.
- Own end-to-end analyses: framing the question with a stakeholder, writing the SQL, modelling the data, building the visualisation, and delivering a recommendation people can act on.
- Build and maintain the dashboards and self-serve tooling (Hex / Amplitude / similar) that stakeholders use weekly, and evolve them as the business evolves.
- Define and document key product and business metrics in partnership with the Analytics Engineering team, so "what does activation mean?" has one canonical answer across Linktree.
- Translate complex findings into plain-language recommendations for non-technical audiences (PMs, marketers, and leaders) through written memos, dashboards, and the occasional well-crafted slide.
- Be a daily user of (and advocate for) good data hygiene: clear metric definitions, reproducible queries, and version-controlled analyses.

## Who We're Looking For
- 2–3 years of experience as a data, business, or product analyst in a fast-moving consumer or B2B product environment.
- SQL-fluent: comfortable with joins, window functions, CTEs, and modelling event-level data into something a stakeholder can actually use. Bonus points for working knowledge of Python (pandas) or dbt.
- Worked with a modern data warehouse (Snowflake, BigQuery, Redshift) and a BI/visualisation tool (Looker, Mode, Tableau, or similar).
- Can take an ambiguous business question, scope it, and deliver a recommendation, not just a chart.
- Strong written and verbal communication skills, and a track record of working closely with non-technical stakeholders.
- Thrive in environments with autonomy and ambiguity, and motivated by your work showing up in real product decisions.
- Can work from one of the SF, LA, Sydney, or Melbourne offices on a hybrid schedule, or fully remote within the listed regions.
